01

Spooner Summit Trailhead

Saturday June 13 — Sunday June 14
Getting There
Address: Spooner Summit Trailhead, Hwy 50 & Hwy 28, Glenbrook, NV 89413
From Santa Rosa: ~3 hrs 45 min via Hwy 12 East → I-80 East → Hwy 267 → Hwy 28 (~210 miles). Straightforward drive through Sacramento and over the Sierra.
From Truckee: ~45 min via Hwy 267 South → Hwy 28 East along the north shore → Hwy 50 East to Spooner Summit (~30 miles). Easy.
From SF Bay Area: ~3.5 hrs via I-80 East to Hwy 267 or Hwy 50 East.
Parking: Free, day-use lot at the summit. Arrive by midday Saturday.
Google Maps: 39.103607, -119.895188 ↗
What to Bring
Pack for 2 nights / 2 days. ~26 miles total over Sat–Sun. Terrain: exposed Nevada ridgeline, Kingsbury area. Elevation ~8,000–9,800 ft.
Need: bear canister, camping permit (dispersed, no fee outside Desolation), trekking poles, microspikes (possible patches), sun protection, layers for cold nights.
Exit Plan
Friends exit Sunday June 14 at Kingsbury South Trailhead or hitch/rideshare back to Spooner (cars parked there). ~5 mi walk to Kingsbury North if doing a one-way. Or arrange a car shuttle between Spooner and Kingsbury South before the weekend.
Nearby Lodging Option
South Lake Tahoe is 20 min south — hotels, Airbnbs, casinos for anyone wanting a bed Friday night before hitting trail Saturday morning.
02

Barker Pass Trailhead

Friday June 19 (Juneteenth) — Sunday June 21
Getting There
Address: Barker Pass Trailhead, Barker Pass Rd, Tahoe City, CA 96145
From Santa Rosa: ~3 hrs 45 min via Hwy 12 East → I-80 East → Hwy 267 → Tahoe City → Hwy 89 South → Barker Pass Rd (~215 miles).
From Truckee: ~35 min via Hwy 267 South → Tahoe City → Hwy 89 South → Barker Pass Rd (~25 miles). Easiest drive of anyone.
From SF Bay Area: ~3.5 hrs via I-80 East → Hwy 267 → Tahoe City → Hwy 89 South → Barker Pass Rd.
Timing: Juneteenth is a federal holiday — most people have Friday off. Drive up Thursday evening or Friday morning to meet Christina as she exits Desolation.
Parking: Barker Pass trailhead has a small lot. Arrive Friday morning. Confirm road is open before driving up — call LTBMU: (530) 543-2600.
Note: If Barker Pass road is still closed (rare in a low snow year), fallback meeting point is Blackwood Canyon Trailhead ~1 mi lower on the same road.
Google Maps: 39.0767895, -120.2352977 ↗
Permits
No Desolation Wilderness permit needed — friends meet Christina at Barker Pass after she exits Desolation. Camping June 19–21 is in Granite Chief Wilderness and National Forest land, which requires no overnight permit. The only permit friends need is a California Campfire Permit (free) to use a stove: readyforwildfire.org ↗
What to Bring
Pack for 2 nights / 3 days (Fri–Sun). ~26 miles total: Barker Pass → Five Lakes → Twin Peaks → Brockway Summit. Beautiful west-side terrain — forgiving, scenic, granite lakes and ridgeline views. Elevation ~6,800–8,400 ft.
Need: bear canister, trekking poles, sun protection, rain layer for afternoon thunderstorms, layers for cold nights. No special permits required — entirely outside Desolation Wilderness.
Exit Plan
Friends exit Sunday June 21 at Brockway Summit Trailhead (Hwy 267, Kings Beach area). Rideshare or car shuttle back to Barker Pass to retrieve cars (~50 min drive).
Brockway GPS: 39.258295, -120.064944 ↗
Nearby Lodging Option
Tahoe City is 20 min north — hotels, Airbnbs, restaurants right on the lake. Book well ahead for Juneteenth weekend.

Car & Transportation
🚗

Christina's Car

Park at 64 Acres Trailhead, Tahoe City (or nearby long-term lot — TRTA advises against extended trailhead parking). Consider parking at a Tahoe City hotel lot or asking your SLT hotel if they'll hold your car. The loop returns to the same point so no shuttle needed.

🤝

Friend Weekend 1 Car Shuttle

Friends park at Spooner Summit (Sat Jun 13). Exit Sunday Jun 14 at Kingsbury South Trailhead. One person drives a car from Spooner to Kingsbury South before the hike starts, retrieved after. Or rideshare (~15 min, ~$20) from Kingsbury back to Spooner.

🤝

Friend Weekend 2 Car Shuttle

Friends park at Barker Pass (Fri Jun 19). Exit Sunday Jun 21 at Brockway Summit Trailhead (Hwy 267). Rideshare or shuttle back to Barker Pass (~50 min drive via Tahoe City → Hwy 89 South → Barker Pass Rd, ~$40–55).

🚌

Getting to Tahoe City

From SF Bay Area: drive ~3.5 hrs via I-80 East to Hwy 267 to Tahoe City, or Hwy 50 East. Amtrak to Truckee + TART bus to Tahoe City also works. North Tahoe Express runs from Reno airport.

Communication & Safety
📡

Cell Service

Unreliable on most of the TRT — especially the east side Nevada sections (Days 2–6) and high ridgelines. Do not count on cell service for navigation or emergencies. Download FarOut maps offline before departing.

🛰️

Satellite Communicator

Strongly recommended given solo hiking on early-season terrain. Garmin inReach Mini 2 is the standard. Rent from REI (~$35/week) or buy. Set up check-in schedule with someone at home — daily or every other day.

🔋

Power

Phone is your primary navigation. Carry a 20,000 mAh power bank — sufficient for 5+ days between the SLT charge stop (Day 8/9). Charge everything fully in SLT before re-entering.

🏥

Emergency Contacts

Leave a full itinerary with a trusted contact at home. Include: start date, route, expected finish, meetup trailhead coordinates, and when to call for help if you miss a check-in.

Water Strategy

East Side Dry Stretches (Days 2–6)

Watson Lake → Gray Lake area (~17 miles)
The longest reliable dry stretch on the entire TRT. Tank up fully at Watson Lake. Carry 4–5L. In a low snow year, some seasonal streams may still be flowing mid-June — check FarOut user reports the day before this section.
Recommended carry capacity
Tahoe City → Watson Lake (~13 miles)
More reliable. Watson Creek typically flowing mid-June. Standard 2L carry sufficient.
Recommended carry capacity
Desolation Wilderness (Days 10–11)
Water everywhere — lakes, streams. Main concern is creek crossings being swollen. Cross in the morning when runoff is lowest. Standard 2L carry sufficient between sources.
