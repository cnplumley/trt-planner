01

Spooner Summit Trailhead

Friday June 13 evening — Sunday June 15
Getting There
Address: Spooner Summit Trailhead, Hwy 50 & Hwy 28, Glenbrook, NV 89413
From SF: ~3.5 hrs via I-80 to Hwy 50 East or I-80 to Hwy 267 to Hwy 28
Parking: Free, day-use lot at the summit. Arrive by 5pm Friday to beat Christina.
Google Maps: 39.103607, -119.895188 ↗
What to Bring
Pack for 2 nights / 3 days. ~26 miles total over Fri–Sun. Terrain: exposed Nevada ridgeline, Kingsbury area, Star Lake. Elevation ~8,000–9,800 ft.
Need: bear canister, camping permit (dispersed, no fee outside Desolation), trekking poles, microspikes (possible patches), sun protection, layers for cold nights.
Exit Plan
Friends exit Sunday June 15 at Kingsbury South Trailhead or hitch/rideshare back to Spooner (cars parked there). ~5 mi walk to Kingsbury North if doing a one-way. Alternatively: arrange a car shuttle between Spooner and Kingsbury South before the weekend.
Nearby Lodging Option
South Lake Tahoe is 20 min south — hotels, Airbnbs, casinos for anyone wanting a bed Friday night before hitting trail Saturday morning.
02

Echo Lake Trailhead

Friday June 20 evening — Sunday June 22
Getting There
Address: Echo Lake Trailhead, Echo Lakes Rd, South Lake Tahoe, CA 95721
From SF: ~3.5 hrs via I-80 to Hwy 50 East, exit Echo Summit (Johnson Pass Rd)
Parking: Limited — arrive early Friday. Day-use lot fills on weekends. Overflow on Echo Lakes Rd.
Note: Echo Chalet seasonal store on-site — snacks, fuel canisters, basic gear.
Google Maps: 38.834834, -120.044231 ↗
What to Bring
Pack for 2 nights / 3 days. ~28 miles total Fri–Sun. Terrain: Five Lakes, Twin Peaks, Brockway area. Elevation ~6,800–8,400 ft. More forgiving than Section 1 weekend.
Need: bear canister, sun protection, rain layer (afternoon thunderstorms). No Desolation permit needed — you're exiting Desolation, not entering.
Exit Plan
Friends exit Sunday June 22 at Brockway Summit Trailhead (Hwy 267, Kings Beach area). Easy drive back to Echo Lake to retrieve cars (~45 min around the lake) or arrange a car shuttle in advance. Brockway to Echo Lake = ~45 min drive.
Brockway GPS: 39.258295, -120.064944 ↗
Nearby Lodging Option
South Lake Tahoe 15 min east — full range of accommodations. Book well ahead for June weekends.

Permits & Reservations
Everything needed before you leave the trailhead

Desolation Wilderness TRT Thru-Hike Permit

Required for your June 19 entry at Echo Lake. Only 10 slots/night — book immediately at recreation.gov/permits/233261 ↗. Entry: Echo Lake. Exit: Meeks Bay. Entry date: 6/19/2026. Exit date: 6/20/2026. Print within 14 days of entry (after June 5).

$16$10/person + $6 fee

California Campfire Permit

Required to use your camp stove anywhere on the TRT — including Nevada sections. Free. Watch a short video and take a quiz at readyforwildfire.org ↗. Print and carry. Expires Dec 31, 2026.

Free

Desolation Wilderness Day Use Permit

Required even for day hikers entering Desolation. Free, self-issued at trailhead kiosks. Your overnight permit covers this — no separate action needed for Christina. Friends joining at Echo Lake on June 20 who enter Desolation briefly should grab a day permit at the Echo Lake trailhead kiosk.

FreeSelf-issue

Barker Pass Road Access — Confirm Open

Road closes seasonally Oct–June. In a low snow year like 2026 it should be open by June 20, but confirm with the Lake Tahoe Basin Management Unit closer to your trip: (530) 543-2600. Fallback: Blackwood Canyon Trailhead (~1 mi lower on same road).

Car & Transportation
🚗

Christina's Car

Park at 64 Acres Trailhead, Tahoe City (or nearby long-term lot — TRTA advises against extended trailhead parking). Consider parking at a Tahoe City hotel lot or asking your SLT hotel if they'll hold your car. The loop returns to the same point so no shuttle needed.

🤝

Friend Weekend 1 Car Shuttle

Friends park at Spooner Summit (Jun 13). To exit at Kingsbury South on Jun 15, one person drives a car from Spooner to Kingsbury South before the hike starts, and another drives it back after. Or: rideshare (~15 min, ~$20) from Kingsbury back to Spooner.

🤝

Friend Weekend 2 Car Shuttle

Friends park at Echo Lake (Jun 20). Exit at Brockway Summit (Jun 22). Someone drives a car from Echo Lake to Brockway before hike, and retrieves it after — or rideshare Echo Lake to Brockway (~45 min drive around the lake, ~$35–50).

🚌

Getting to Tahoe City

From SF Bay Area: drive ~3.5 hrs via I-80 East to Hwy 267 to Tahoe City, or Hwy 50 East. Amtrak to Truckee + TART bus to Tahoe City also works. North Tahoe Express runs from Reno airport.

Resupply
🛒

South Lake Tahoe — Day 8/9

Rideshare ~9 miles from Echo Summit/Echo Lake trailhead. Do not road walk — dangerous, no shoulder.

  • Grocery: Raley's, Safeway, Grocery Outlet
  • Gear: Alpenglow Sports, Tahoe Mountain Sports
  • Fuel canisters available at gear shops
  • Consider mailing a resupply box to your hotel
🏪

Tahoe City — Start/Finish

Trail runs through town. Save Mart grocery nearly on the TRT. Post office ~1 mile for mail drops.

  • Fuel canisters at Alpenglow Sports
  • Multiple restaurants for pre/post-hike meals
  • Bear canister rental: TRTA (775) 298-4485
Communication & Safety
📡

Cell Service

Unreliable on most of the TRT — especially the east side Nevada sections (Days 2–6) and high ridgelines. Do not count on cell service for navigation or emergencies. Download FarOut maps offline before departing.

🛰️

Satellite Communicator

Strongly recommended given solo hiking on early-season terrain. Garmin inReach Mini 2 is the standard. Rent from REI (~$35/week) or buy. Set up check-in schedule with someone at home — daily or every other day.

🔋

Power

Phone is your primary navigation. Carry a 20,000 mAh power bank — sufficient for 5+ days between the SLT charge stop (Day 8/9). Charge everything fully in SLT before re-entering.

🏥

Emergency Contacts

Leave a full itinerary with a trusted contact at home. Include: start date, route, expected finish, meetup trailhead coordinates, and when to call for help if you miss a check-in.

Water Strategy

East Side Dry Stretches (Days 2–6)

Watson Lake → Gray Lake area (~17 miles)
The longest reliable dry stretch on the entire TRT. Tank up fully at Watson Lake. Carry 4–5L. In a low snow year, some seasonal streams may still be flowing mid-June — check FarOut user reports the day before this section.
Recommended carry capacity
Tahoe City → Watson Lake (~13 miles)
More reliable. Watson Creek typically flowing mid-June. Standard 2L carry sufficient.
Recommended carry capacity
Desolation Wilderness (Days 10–11)
Water everywhere — lakes, streams. Main concern is creek crossings being swollen. Cross in the morning when runoff is lowest. Standard 2L carry sufficient between sources.
